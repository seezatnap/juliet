# The Waking Wood

The fern uncurls its fist beside the stream,
The mossy stones hold fast their ancient keep,
And every bough that bends toward the gleam
Recalls the winter it was told to sleep.

The wren alights and flings a silver call
Across the clearing misted with the dew,
While deer emerge along the mossy wall

So turns the wood from silence into song,
