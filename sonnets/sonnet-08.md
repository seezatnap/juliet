# The Cartography of Stars

When darkness falls the ancient atlas spreads
Its silver script across the waiting sky,
And every star that burns above our heads
Has traveled longer than a human cry.

We named them once for gods and beasts of old,
Drew lines between them like a child's connect,
And told ourselves the stories they had told
Were ours to keep, and ours to recollect.

But stars care nothing for the myths we weave;
They fuse and fail by laws we barely know,
And all the constellations we believe
Are accidents of angle, depth, and glow.
